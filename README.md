# Strategic HRM Audit & Institutional Field Study: Millat Tractors Limited

##  Project Overview
This repository contains a comprehensive Human Resource Management (HRM) capstone project evaluating the enterprise-level workforce management systems at **Millat Tractors Limited (MTL)** Pakistan’s leading agricultural machinery manufacturer commanding a 70% market share. 

The study bridges theoretical HR frameworks with industrial reality by contrasting MTL’s current operational workflows against established literature by **Gary Dessler, Herman Aguinis, and Raymond Noe**. The analysis covers talent acquisition governance, industrial relations, performance management, and organizational culture.

##  Key Research Findings

### 1. Talent Acquisition & Structural Governance
* **Workforce Planning:** Workforce expansion is strictly regulated via the official enterprise organogram. To ensure lean corporate operations, any new headcount requires strategic board-level approval prior to recruitment.
* **Sourcing Strategy:** MTL balances internal institutional mobility with external talent pools by deploying a dual-channel funnel utilizing targeted national newspaper advertisements and Internal Job Postings (IJP).

### 2. Performance Management & Total Rewards
* **Evaluation Metrics:** Employee appraisal is structured through objective, KPI-linked financial reward frameworks and traditional annual review cycles.
* **Holistic Welfare Culture:** Deeply rooted in its historic 1992 employee buyout, MTL maintains a robust psychological contract with its workforce by offering high-impact benefits, including children's educational subsidies, marriage grants, and compassionate family financial support.
* **Industrial Relations:** Effectively manages a lean core workforce of 460+ direct employees alongside complex collective bargaining dynamics through a dedicated Industrial Relations unit.

---

##  Strategic Recommendations for HR Transformation

To transition MTL’s highly stable operational model into a modern **High-Performance Work System (HPWS)**, this study proposed two primary strategic interventions:

1. **Agile Performance Loops:** Shift from rigid, retroactive annual appraisal cycles to a model of frequent, continuous, and real-time feedback loops to enhance workforce responsiveness and psychological safety.
2. **Advanced Training Evaluation (ROI):** Transition past standard post-training satisfaction surveys by implementing the **Kirkpatrick Four-Level Training Evaluation Model** to quantify the behavioral and organizational impact of employee development initiatives.
